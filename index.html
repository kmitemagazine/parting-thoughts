PARTING THOUGHTS

We asked 4th years of kmit some questions. Here are their responses

A precipice is a “very steep rock face or cliff” that protrudes from high rocky structures. It’s also where the hard ground is still beneath your feet and the cold wind is pressing at your face. Standing at the precipice feels terrifying but also thrilling. Graduating from undergraduate college feels a lot like standing at the precipice. The anticipation of whether taking flight means rising higher or crashing into lower elevations can cause one to look back — for the safe, the familiar, the yesterday. As another batch of young students filled with dreams and the potential to achieve them departs from the ever-so-closed gates of KMIT, we thought it would be a fun exercise to ask them what “the safe, the familiar, the yesterday” consists of. We sent something of a questionnaire to a few students (because an interview was impossible to fit into the busy lives of us, students) and here are a few answers to them.
